# Source trust policy

Apply this authority order:

1. `primary`: legislation, articles, notes, and official judicial decisions.
2. `secondary`: explanatory or reference material that is not itself binding law.
3. `auxiliary`: public Dadrah questions, answers, tags, and lawyer profiles.

Primary sources support legal conclusions. Secondary sources may explain a primary source but must not silently replace one. Auxiliary material may show a practical question or viewpoint, but must never be described as law, precedent, an official interpretation, or proof that a rule exists.

When sources conflict, state the conflict and prefer the more authoritative and more specific source. Do not infer that a law is current merely because it was retrieved.
